Malcolm X

Malcolm X in 1925, in Omaha, Nebraska. His father was a Baptist preacher and follower of Marcus Garvey, his entire family moved to Lansing, Michigan. Malcolm was very intelligent and a good student, but unfortunately he dropped out of school after the eighth grade. Over time, Malcolm's occupation was to be a writer, minister, spokesperson and human rights activist. He was also placed in foster care and his mother suffered a mental breakdown. He began wearing zoot suits, dealing drugs, earned the nickname "Detroit Red" and at age 21 went to prison for robbery. He later organized temples, founded a newspaper, and ran Temple No.7 in Harlem, New York City, and was named the national representative of Islam by Elijah Muhammad, the second most powerful position in the NOI.
